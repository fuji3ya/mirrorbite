3. What Mirrorbite does

  • Accepts a meal photo from your iPhone camera or library.
  • Sends the photo encrypted over HTTPS to our analysis proxy, which queries a generative AI model (Gemini 2.5 Flash, with Claude Sonnet 4 as fallback).
  • Returns a structured JSON result: a 0–100 directional index, three categorical nutritional axes (good / caution / low), one strength bullet, one improvement bullet, the identified dish, and a confidence level.
  • If the AI is unsure, the app returns a "withheld" judgment with a reason (e.g., photo unclear, non-food, multiple meals). This is intentional and is not a malfunction.

4. What Mirrorbite does NOT do

  • It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
  • It does not provide specific calorie counts, gram amounts, or macronutrient breakdowns. We return categorical levels only.
  • It does not detect allergens or dietary restrictions reliably. Do not rely on the app for allergy avoidance.
  • It does not store, share, or use your photos for any purpose other than the single analysis call.

6. AI limitations and accuracy

Photo-only nutritional estimation has fundamental accuracy limitations. We have deliberately designed Mirrorbite to:

  • Refuse to output gram amounts or calorie counts (we never invent specific numbers).
  • Withhold judgment when the photo is unclear, non-food, or shows multiple meals.
  • Use range expressions ("noticeably", "meaningfully") rather than pinpoint deltas in next-meal suggestions.

Despite this design, results may be inaccurate. You are responsible for evaluating whether Mirrorbite's output is appropriate for your own dietary needs. If you have any health condition, are pregnant, breastfeeding, taking medication, or under the care of a clinician, consult that clinician before changing your diet based on app output.
